Privacy Policy

Last updated: June 22, 2026

This Privacy Policy explains what information Carlyze ("the app", "we", "us") collects, how it's used, and your choices. Carlyze is developed by Lukas Stanko.

Information we collect

  • Account information. When you sign in with Google, we receive your name, email address, and profile picture from Google to create and identify your account.
  • Car and garage data. Details you enter about cars you're inspecting (make, model, mileage, fuel type, etc.), the analyses generated from them, notes you write, and the status you assign to saved cars in your Garage.
  • Usage events. Basic in-app actions (e.g. signing in, saving a car, updating a status, completing an analysis) tied to your account, used to understand how the app is used and to improve it.

How we use this information

We use the information above solely to operate and improve Carlyze: to authenticate you, sync your Garage and analyses across sessions, and to understand which features are useful. We do not sell your personal information, and we do not share it with third parties for advertising purposes.

Where data is stored

Your data is stored using Google Firebase (Authentication and Firestore database), a third-party infrastructure provider. Firebase's own privacy and security practices apply to data in transit and at rest on their infrastructure.

Data retention and deletion

We retain your account and garage data for as long as your account is active. You can request deletion of your account and all associated data at any time by emailing us — see Contact below. We will process deletion requests within a reasonable timeframe.

Children's privacy

Carlyze is not directed at children under 13, and we do not knowingly collect personal information from children under 13.

Changes to this policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time. Material changes will be reflected by updating the "Last updated" date above.
